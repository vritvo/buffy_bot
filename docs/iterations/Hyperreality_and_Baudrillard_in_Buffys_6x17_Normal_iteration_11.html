Detailed Comments

The paper's central thesis that 'Normal Again' operates within Baudrillard's framework of simulation where distinctions between real and copy become meaningless is fundamentally flawed. Baudrillard's hyperreality describes a condition where simulation precedes and determines reality, but this episode maintains clear ontological hierarchies - one reality must be 'true' and the other 'false.' The asylum sequences are presented as revelatory interruptions, not as equally valid simulations. The paper's strongest insights concern Buffy's agency and choice, particularly the Nietzschean reading of her decision as life affirmation regardless of ontological status. However, these insights are undermined by the misapplied theoretical framework. The analysis would be much stronger if it focused on the psychological and existential dimensions rather than forcing a postmodern reading that the text doesn't support.
