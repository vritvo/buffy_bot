Detailed Comments

The paper's central thesis that Buffy systematically dismantles soul-based morality in favor of constructivist ethics is intriguing but poorly supported. The paper would benefit from more careful textual analysis, engagement with counterarguments, and a more nuanced understanding of how the show balances essentialist and constructivist elements rather than claiming it rejects one entirely.

Most problematically, the paper attributes dialogue to Dawn ('Spike has a chip. Same diff') that does not appear in the provided scripts and seems to be a misremembering or fabrication. This is particularly damaging since this quote is presented as a 'pivotal argument' and 'devastating observation' central to the thesis. The paper would benefit from more rigorous textual analysis, focusing on what the characters actually say rather than what the author wishes they had said. Additionally, while the philosophical framework is sophisticated, the application feels somewhat forced - the Wittgensteinian 'beetle in a box' analogy, while clever, doesn't fully capture how souls function narratively in the Buffyverse. The paper also doesn't adequately grapple with counterevidence, such as Angel's clear moral transformation following soul restoration, which suggests the show maintains some essentialist elements even in later seasons. A stronger paper would focus more narrowly on specific episodes and characters with detailed textual analysis rather than attempting such a sweeping philosophical argument.
