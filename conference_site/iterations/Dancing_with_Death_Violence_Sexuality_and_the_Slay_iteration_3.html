Detailed Comments

This paper tackles an important and compelling thesis about Season 5's treatment of mortality and authentic existence, and the central argument about Spike's death wish analysis proving prophetic is genuinely insightful. However, the work is significantly undermined by factual errors in script citations. Most critically, the paper attributes 'You're beneath me' to Buffy speaking to Spike, when the actual script shows this line spoken by Cecily to William in the 1880 flashback. This error is particularly damaging because it's central to the paper's argument about power dynamics and psychological positioning. Additionally, while the philosophical framework drawing on concepts of authentic existence is sophisticated, it needs better anchoring in specific textual evidence. The paper would benefit from more careful verification of all quotes against the actual scripts and a more systematic analysis of how the philosophical concepts emerge from the text itself rather than being applied to it.
